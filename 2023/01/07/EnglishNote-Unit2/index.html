Unit 2

slender: 单词意义的颜色：褒义词和贬义词

slender
adj. gracefully thin 苗条的

thin
瘦的(贬义) She’s very pretty but she’s too thin. 她很漂亮，但是她太瘦

skinny
皮包骨的，贬义 I don’t like his looks – he’s too skinny. 我不喜欢他的样子——他太瘦了

bony
皮包骨的，贬义 He held up his bony fingers. 他举起他那瘦骨嶙峋的手指

a bag of bones
皮包骨的，贬义 She has lost so much weight that she’s just turning into a bag of bones. 她瘦了很多，简直成了皮包骨。

slim: quite thin in a way that is attractive
苗条的

lean: slim and strong
瘦而强壮的 Long‐distance runners are usually fairly lean. 长跑运动员通常很瘦。

petite: short and slim
娇小玲珑的

underweight:
(中性)体重不足的 He is only slightly underweight for his height. 按他的身高来说，他体重仅稍轻了一点儿。

不胖不瘦的

  • 女: well‐proportionated 匀称的 with a well‐proportioned figure
  • 男: fit 健壮的 with a strong build

超重

  • fat: 胖的(最直接，最伤人)
  • stout: rather fat 胖胖的 He was a tall, stout man with gray hair. 他是一个身材高大、体格粗壮、头发灰白的人。
  • stocky: strong and wide 粗壮的(稍微好一点点，仍然不是褒义词) He’s got the stocky build of a rugby player. 他有着橄榄球运动员一样的粗壮体型。
  • solid: 敦实的 As a child, James was always quite solid. James 儿时很敦实。
  • big: 个子大(几乎是中性词) Sophie didn’t use to be so big, did she? 索菲以前个子没这么大，是吗?
  • plump, chubby: slightly fat 丰满的;胖乎乎的 (褒义词) She was admiring the baby’s plump little legs. 她在欣赏婴儿小小的、胖乎乎的腿。Look at the lovely chubby cheeks! 看看那可爱的胖乎乎的脸蛋!
  • overweight I’m overweight by 2kg according to my doctor. 据医生说，我超重2公斤。
  • obese (医学用词)有肥胖症的

historic: -ic与-ical的意义区别

  • historic: famous or important in history, or potentially so 历史上著名的，历史上重大的，历史性的 a time of historic change 历史转折
  • historical: concerning past events历史(上)的a historical novel 历史小说

‐ic and ‐ical

  • ‐ic: 紧靠该名词(或其引申的价值)的，关系直
  • ical 关于或有关该名词的，关系稍微疏远一些
  • economic:经济的，经济学的 – economic development, economic crisis
  • economical:考虑经济方面的，节约的，划得来的 (“经济”小吃) an economical housewife, an economical car, an economical style of writing
  • poetic:有诗意的(尤指优美的)，诗歌价值的 – poetic language 像诗一样美丽的语言
  • poetical:诗体的，非散文体的,诗歌形式的 – poetical language 非散文体的语言
  • electric:发电的，电动的，于电直接相关的, an electric generator 发电机, an electric car 电动车,
  • electrical与电有关的,电气的 an electrical engineer 电气工程师

but: 双重否定的理解，意群的划分

  • He is nothing but a singer. 他只不过是个歌手而已!
  • He is anything but a singer. 他绝对不是歌手!

有语法家把but做关系代词，引导定于从句， 意思为: that(which,who)… not… 此时主句往往是否定句，全句为双重否定。There is no man but feels pity for starving children. There is nothing in the world but teaches us some lessons. There is no book in the reading book but I have read.

由此拓展开来，but用于否定句后面，很多都表示再否定，即: 而不，而非，构成双重否定。It never rains but pours.

不要死记硬背，要理解

log：意义获取的理据以及文化差异对比喻喻体选择的影响

n.

  • 原木; 圆材 Some birds like to nest in a hollow log, log cabin, sleep like a log
  • 日志
  • 观察记录
    v.
  • 砍伐 Very few trees in this area are suitable for logging. 这里没有几颗树适合砍伐。
  • 记载, 记录 They have kept a detailed log of your activities.
  • log on/in, log off/out
    一根长的麻绳一端绑根木头，一端系在沙漏上，中间打上等距的节。航行 时，把木头从船尾扔进海里，翻转沙漏，等沙漏漏完后收回木板头，查看有多少个节被打湿，推算船只速度。这也是船只速度单位——节kn, knot的来历。

sleep like a log 中西文化差异对比喻喻体选择的影响很大，尤其注意:

  • eat like a horse(eat too much)
  • eat like a bird (eat too little)
  • as busy as a bee [spinning top]
  • as poor as a church mouse
  • cast pearls before swine (waste something good on one who has no appreciation for it)
  • as wet as a drowned rat
  • as strong as a horse
  • 无风不起浪 (Where there is a wave, there is a wind!) Where there is smoke, there is fire.
  • 巧妇难为无米之炊 (Even a smart wife cannot cook without rice.) One cannot make bricks without straw.
  • 这山望见那山高 The grass is always greener on the other side of the fence.

lot：词义获取的理据–抽签

  • a lot of (lots of): a large number or amount of (sb/sth) 大量; 许多:
    lot来自古英语hlot, 意为“决定一个人的份额的物体”，多为骰子、抽签用的麦草，更常见的是刻有名字的木条。
  • draw lots, cast lots 抽签 we drew lots to decide the order. 我们抽签来决定顺序。These officers were elected rather than selected by lot. 官员们是通过选举而不是抽签选上的。
  • lottery 彩票，博彩
  • 后指通过抽签而给与(或获得)之物->往往是一片土地 a vacant lot 一块空地皮, parking lot
  • 抽签要抽中是有几率的，故lot指机遇,几率,可能性,命运。 Her lot has been a hard one. 她命苦。I would not want to share his lot. 我可不愿和他同甘共苦。 He made the best of his lot and worked diligently. 他利用命运给他的一切机会努力工作。
  • 抽签的过程是把许多签放在一起，供抽签人来抽，所以lot指放在一起的许多东西, 大量，许多. a lot of, lots of; Nobody in the first lot of applicants was suitable for the job. 在第一批求职者中无人适合做那份工作。
  • -> (出售物品的)项目, 批(尤指拍卖物) Nineteen lots of the auction failed to sell. 19件拍卖品没有成交。

compel: 词根词缀学习与拓展

使(某人)做某事; 强迫 We cannot compel you to (do it), but we think you should do it. 我们不能强迫你(去做), 但认为你应该做。
-> 不能用于被动句:强夺、 强求(某物); 强迫; 使必须 You can compel obedience, but you can never compel affection.
你可以逼人服从, 却无法逼人生爱。 Circumstances have compelled a change of plan. 因环境所迫, 计划已经改变。 His courage compels universal admiration. 他的勇气不禁令人肃然起敬。

组成分析:
com‐: together, company, communicate, common
‐pel‐: to force, to drive 迫 drive together

repel 排斥 Like charges repel each other, and unlike charges attract each other. 同性电荷互相排斥，异性电荷互相吸引。 Like poles of magnets repel. 相同的磁极互相排斥。

propel drive forward向前推动, 驱动
pro‐:forward
The boat is(was) propelled by using a very long paddle. 这条船是靠长桨推进的。 His addiction to drugs propelled him towards a way of crime. 他吸毒成瘾使他走上了犯罪的道路。

expel 驱逐; 赶走; 开除
ex‐ out
drive out Two attachés at the embassy were expelled from the country. 大使馆的两名随员已被驱逐出境。

dispel: 驱赶，驱逐，驱散，使……消失(如情感，怀疑，信念等)
dis‐ apart
He tried all means to dispel his doubts but failed. 他想方设法消除他的疑虑，但失败了。
